Findings
01
Mobile Add-to-Cart Failure
Mobile users recorded zero add-to-cart events from February 5th onward. In the pre-anomaly period, mobile accounted for 21% of total revenue — that contribution dropped to 0% during the anomaly period. Notably, View Item still fires on mobile, pinpointing the break at or immediately before the cart action.

Tablet users showed a concurrent uplift. This contrast raises a pointed question: was there a product or UX update that inadvertently improved the tablet experience while breaking the mobile one?
Mobile Revenue: $0 −$8,685 vs pre Tablet CVR: +17.7%
02
CPC Attribution Breakdown
Google CPC traffic fell by 92% during the anomaly period, while direct traffic surged by 281% — a near-textbook signal of UTM parameter failure, where paid sessions are being misclassified as direct. The elevated CVR on the remaining CPC sessions (12.9%) suggests the residual volume is real, high-intent traffic.

Critically, the drop is consistent across device categories and operating systems including Android and Windows, which rules out iOS privacy features (ITP) as a contributing factor. This points to a systemic tagging issue upstream — likely Google Ads auto-tagging configuration.
CPC Sessions: −92% Direct: +281% ROAS untrustworthy
Recommendations
Priority Issue Action
🔴 Critical
Mobile add-to-cart broken
 QA the add-to-cart button on mobile across browsers and OS versions. Determine whether the button is non-functional or has a visibility/UX issue. Cross-reference with any code or product releases made around Feb 5th.
🔴 Critical
CPC traffic untagged
 Audit Google Ads auto-tagging settings. Verify that UTM parameters are being appended correctly at the campaign level. Without reliable attribution, ROAS cannot be accurately calculated.
🟡 Preventive
UTM single point of failure
 Add manual UTM parameter backups to all paid search ads. Auto-tagging should not be the single point of failure for attribution — hardcoded UTMs act as a reliable fallback.
🟡 Preventive
No channel-level anomaly alerts
 Set up anomaly alerts for channel-level traffic shifts (e.g., >50% week-over-week change in any source/medium) to catch attribution issues before they compound.
🟡 Preventive
No mobile funnel monitoring
 Implement a dedicated alert for mobile add-to-cart rate. A mobile-specific funnel monitor would have surfaced this issue within hours of February 5th rather than allowing it to persist for weeks.
Methodology
1
Baseline Establishment
Overall KPIs reviewed for the full date range, then split into pre-anomaly and anomaly periods to quantify the performance gap. Revenue per session used as the primary impact metric.
2
Funnel Diagnostics
KPI deterioration pointed to a drop-off between the PDP and add-to-cart step. The sharpest funnel break at View Item → Add to Cart (−35.7%) narrowed the investigation scope.
3
Segmentation Analysis
Data segmented by device category and traffic source/medium, revealing two independent but concurrent anomalies: a mobile UX failure and a CPC attribution breakdown. Supporting queries in Queries.sql.
